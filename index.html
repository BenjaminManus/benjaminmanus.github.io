About Me

Hello, and welcome to my portfolio. My name is Benjamin Manus and I'm a front and back-end web developer. I spend most of my days, practically every day, experimenting with HTML5, CSS3, and JavaScript; dabbling with Python and Ruby on Rails; and inhaling a wide variety of potentially useless information through a few hundred RSS feeds. I build websites that delight and inform. I do it well. I’m curious, and I enjoy work that challenges me to learn something new and stretch in a different direction. I do my best to stay on top of changes in the state of the art so that I can meet challenges with tools well suited to the job at hand. I'm also the founder and Chief Executive Officer of Westfield Web Design which benefits the Westfield Academy Model United Nations program.

Other than web design and development for clients, I give online courses on how to become a web developer, or in some cases a better web developer, via Google Hangouts. I enjoy working with students and I love seeing what they come up with for their capstone project at the end of the session.

Education

Westfield Academy

Debater and Model UN Student • September 2013

I joined the Westfield Academy of Debate and Public Speaking shortly after I finished intermediate school. Hands down the best decision I've made. The Westfield Academy debate team has won numerous awards at tournements such as the Loomis-Chaffee Prepared Policy competition, the State Middle School and State High School competitions, as well as others.

Udemy

HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript, and Ruby • May 2011

Udemy is where I learned most of my computer programming and website development skills. Codeconstant

JavaScript Instructor • December 2014 - Present

Codeconstant is yet another site where you can learn how to build your programming skills. Codeconstant focuses on teaching enrollees how to create iOS and Android applications and at the end of the year, students can upload their projects to the Apple App Store or the Android Store at a small fee. I love working with the students and guiding them on what looks good and what doesn't as well as teaching them new techniques to make the application development process quicker and easier.
